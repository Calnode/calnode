# Calnode Trademark & Naming Policy

Calnode's **code** is open source under [Apache-2.0](LICENSE) — you're free to use, modify,
fork, self-host, and redistribute it. The Apache license covers the *code*; it does **not**
grant rights to the **"Calnode" name or logo**. This short policy explains how to use the name
so users can always tell the official project and its maintainers' offerings apart from forks
and third-party services.

"Calnode" and the Calnode logo are marks of the Calnode project (Wynne Pirini and its
successors and assigns). This isn't about restricting the software — it's standard practice
for open-source projects (Grafana, GitLab, and others do the same) to keep the *name* trustworthy.

## You may, without asking

- State that your product **"uses Calnode"**, is **"built on Calnode"**, or is
  **"compatible with Calnode"** — accurate, factual references.
- Use the name in blog posts, talks, tutorials, comparisons, and documentation.
- Run your own self-hosted instance of Calnode for yourself or your organization.
- Fork the code and build on it.

## Please do **not**, without written permission

- Name your fork, product, or **hosted/commercial service "Calnode"** (or a confusingly
  similar name, or a name incorporating "Calnode" such as "Calnode Cloud", "Calnode Pro",
  "HostedCalnode"). Give your distribution its **own distinct name**.
- Use the Calnode name or logo in a way that implies **official status, endorsement, or
  affiliation** with the Calnode project when none exists.
- Use the Calnode logo as your product's logo, or modify it and present it as Calnode's.
- Register domain names, social handles, or trademarks containing "Calnode".

## Forks & redistribution

You're welcome to fork and redistribute the code (that's the Apache license). If you
distribute a **modified** version, please give it a different name and make clear it's not the
official Calnode — e.g. *"FooScheduler, a fork of Calnode."* This keeps users from mistaking a
fork for the official project or its maintainers' services.

## Questions / permission

For anything not covered here, or to request permission for a specific use, contact the
maintainers (open an issue or email the address in the repository). We're reasonable —
this policy exists to prevent confusion and impersonation, not to stop legitimate use.
